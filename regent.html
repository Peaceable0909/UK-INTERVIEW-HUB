Module 1 — Preparation

Complete Interview Guide

Everything you need to know, in the order you should learn it. Read all sections.

A credibility interview is the university's way of checking that you are genuinely prepared for your studies, understand what you are coming to study, and can realistically support yourself in the UK. The goal is not to trick you — they just want to be sure you know the basics about your course, accommodation, finances, future plans, and why you chose Regent.

If you answer confidently, naturally, and truthfully, you will pass. Your answers should sound like things you discovered through your own research, not like a memorised script.

verified Final tips to pass

Speak slowly and calmly. Do not rush. Answer naturally, as if talking to a real person, not reading from memory. Be honest about your finances and your intentions at all times. Show that you researched Regent and London life fully. Stay consistent — every answer must match what is on your application.

Module 4 — Response Library

Sample Natural Answers

Model answers in a calm, natural style. Adapt them with your own details — never recite word for word.

Q

Why did you choose the UK for your studies?

"I chose the UK because the education system is practical, respected worldwide, and allows students to gain real career skills. The UK also has a diverse environment, so it is easy to meet people from different cultures. I felt the teaching style would help me grow academically and professionally."
tips_and_updatesAdd something specific to your field — for example, "The UK is a global hub for finance / technology / healthcare, which is directly relevant to my career goals."
Q

Why Regent College London specifically?

"I chose Regent because of their small class sizes and strong student support system. The 93% satisfaction rate and the fact that degrees are awarded by a well-ranked UK university gave me confidence in the quality of the programme. I also liked the Thinking Into Character leadership programme and the free laptop loan — it shows the college genuinely invests in its students beyond the classroom."
tips_and_updatesMake it personal. Mention something specific you found during your research — for example, a particular module, the campus location, or the student development services.
Q

How does your course relate to your background and previous experience?

"My previous degree was in [subject] and I have worked in [field] for [X] years. This course builds directly on that foundation — particularly the modules on [Module A] and [Module B], which cover areas I want to develop further. The combination of theory and practical application in this programme is exactly what I need to move into [next career step]."
tips_and_updatesFill in the brackets with your real details. Connect at least two specific modules to your actual background. Be concrete, not vague.
Q

Where will you be staying and how much will it cost?

"I will be living in [area, e.g. Wembley], with a postcode of [e.g. HA9 7XX]. I researched accommodation on SpareRoom and found a room for approximately £[750] per month. I chose this area because it is close to my campus, well-connected by the London Underground, and the cost fits within my monthly budget."
tips_and_updatesYou must have a specific area and postcode ready. Research SpareRoom now and pick a realistic option near your campus.
Q

How will you fund your studies?

"My studies are funded by [myself / my family / my sponsor]. The total tuition fee is £[X]. I estimate my monthly living costs at around £[1,300], covering rent of £[750], food of approximately £[250], transport of around £[130], and other utilities and expenses. I currently have savings of £[X] which I have already converted to GBP using Oanda. My [parent / sponsor] also earns approximately [£X / local currency equivalent] per year and will contribute to my living costs. I also have a contingency fund of £[X] for unexpected expenses."
tips_and_updatesThe more specific your figures, the more credible you sound. Know the exact tuition fee. Know the exchange rate. Have the breakdown ready.
Q

What are your goals after graduation?

"In the short term, I plan to return to [home country] and apply for a position as [job title] at companies such as [Company A] or [Company B]. I expect a starting salary in the range of [local currency equivalent]. In the long term, I want to [e.g. move into a management role / establish my own business] within five years. This course will give me the specific skills in [area] that are currently in high demand in my country."
tips_and_updatesThe interviewer needs to be confident you plan to return home. State this clearly and give a concrete plan for what you will do when you get back.
Q

Do you understand your visa responsibilities?

"Yes. As an international student on a student visa, I know I can work a maximum of 20 hours per week during term time and full-time during official university holidays. I must attend all scheduled classes and lectures. I am required to notify the university if I change my address. I also understand that I cannot claim public funds during my stay in the UK. I take these responsibilities seriously."
tips_and_updatesSay this clearly and without hesitation. If you stumble on the working hours limit (20 hours), the interviewer will notice. Know it by heart.
