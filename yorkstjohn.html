Module 1 — Preparation

Complete Interview Guide

Everything you need to know, in the order you should learn it. Read all sections.

The Pre-CAS interview is designed to confirm that you are a genuine student, have researched your course and university, and are prepared academically, financially, and personally for your studies in the UK. Your interview will be recorded and assessed before your CAS (Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies) is issued.

If you answer confidently, naturally, and truthfully, you will pass. Your answers should sound like things you discovered through your own research, not like a memorised script. The interviewer is checking you are a genuine student with clear academic and career motivation.

verified Final tips to pass

Review your Statement of Purpose (SOP) before the interview. Answer clearly and confidently — avoid memorised scripts. Ensure a quiet environment, stable internet, and professional appearance. Prepare to speak naturally in English; your fluency is part of the assessment. Most importantly: show genuine enthusiasm for your course and university. Stay calm during follow-up questions and maintain eye contact.

Module 4 — Response Library

Sample Natural Answers

Model answers in a calm, natural style. Adapt them with your own details — never recite word for word.

Q

Why did you choose to study in the UK?

"I chose the UK because of the international recognition of UK degrees, the shorter course duration of one year for masters, and the quality of teaching. The UK education system is intensive and globally respected. I also considered the post-study work opportunities available in the UK compared to other countries like the USA, which has higher costs, or Australia, which has more work restrictions."
tips_and_updatesCompare UK with other countries you considered. Mention specific advantages like duration, cost, or work opportunities.
Q

Why did you choose York St John University specifically?

"I compared York St John with Northumbria and Sheffield Hallam. Northumbria offered a similar MSc, but I preferred YSJ because of its smaller cohort sizes, which I believe will allow me to receive more personalised feedback. Sheffield Hallam's course didn't include the leadership and professional development modules that YSJ offers. I also liked that the York campus is in a quieter, student-friendly city, which suits my study style. YSJ is also ranked in the top 10 for student satisfaction."
tips_and_updatesName 2-3 other universities you compared YSJ with. Give specific reasons — rankings, course structure, teaching quality, location, or facilities.
Q

Why did you choose this programme?

"I chose the MSc International Business at YSJ because of modules like Global Strategic Management and Business Analytics. In my final year of my BBA, I focused my dissertation on how SMEs use data to develop marketing strategies, which made me realise I want stronger analytical and global decision-making skills. The course's CMI-accredited leadership content will help me progress into an international business development role."
tips_and_updatesConnect specific modules to your previous study or work experience. Explain how this fills a gap in your skills or knowledge.
Q

What modules will you study?

"I will study Corporate Finance, Risk Management, and Research Methods. I am particularly interested in Risk Management because it will help me understand how to assess financial uncertainties in international markets. I also look forward to the Research Methods module as it will prepare me for my dissertation."
tips_and_updatesName at least 2-3 correct modules from your course. Briefly explain why one interests you specifically.
Q

Where will you stay in the UK?

"I plan to stay in university-managed accommodation at The Grange, which costs approximately £129 per week. It is less than 1 mile from campus and is self-catered with single occupancy rooms. I have researched this thoroughly and it fits my budget well. The accommodation includes contents insurance and is covered by 24/7 security."
tips_and_updatesFor York: mention specific accommodation name, weekly cost, and distance. For London: mention private accommodation as there is no university-provided accommodation.
Q

How much is your tuition fee?

"My tuition fee is £11,800 for the full programme. I have already paid the £5,900 deposit, and the remaining £5,900 will be paid in instalments. I have the funds available in my bank account and can show the bank statements as proof."
tips_and_updatesKnow the exact figure. State how much you've paid and how much remains. Mention you have proof of funds available.
Q

Who is sponsoring you?

"My father is sponsoring me. He is a civil engineer earning approximately £45,000 per year. He has been saving for my education for the past three years and has sufficient funds in his account to cover my tuition and living costs. I can provide his employment letter and bank statements as evidence."
tips_and_updatesState the relationship clearly. Mention their occupation and approximate income. Confirm you have documentary evidence.
Q

What are your plans after graduation?

"I will return to Nigeria to work in investment banking. I plan to apply to companies like Stanbic IBTC or Access Bank in their corporate finance divisions. This course will give me the international business knowledge and analytical skills that are in high demand in Nigeria's growing financial sector. I expect a starting salary of approximately ₦8-10 million per annum."
tips_and_updatesBe specific about returning home. Name actual companies and job roles. Explain how the course qualifies you for these roles.
